Introduction

Employee attrition, or turnover, is a critical metric for organizations as it impacts productivity, morale, and overall business performance. This report aims to analyze the factors contributing to employee attrition and provide insights to help the company develop strategies to improve employee retention.

Data Collection

The dataset used for this analysis was sourced from the 'greendestination.csv' file. It contains various features related to employee demographics, job satisfaction, compensation, and other relevant attributes.

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

EDA was conducted to understand the distribution of data, identify missing values, and explore relationships between different features and attrition.

Key Findings:

  • Attrition Rate: The overall attrition rate is calculated and displayed, showing the percentage of employees who left the company.
  • Attrition due to Age: Younger employees tend to have higher attrition rates, indicating that age is a significant factor in employee turnover.
  • Years at Company vs. Attrition: Employees with fewer years at the company are more likely to leave, suggesting that newer employees are at a higher risk of attrition.
  • Distance from Home vs. Attrition: Employees living farther from the workplace tend to have higher attrition rates, highlighting the impact of commute distance on employee retention.
  • Job Satisfaction vs. Attrition: Lower job satisfaction is associated with higher attrition rates, emphasizing the importance of job satisfaction in retaining employees.
  • Monthly Income vs. Attrition: Employees with lower monthly incomes are more likely to leave, indicating that compensation plays a crucial role in employee retention.
  • Years with Current Manager vs. Attrition: Employees with fewer years with their current manager tend to have higher attrition rates, suggesting that managerial relationships influence employee turnover.
  • Years Since Last Promotion vs. Attrition: Employees who have not been promoted for a long time are more likely to leave, pointing to the importance of career advancement opportunities in retaining employees.
  • Gender vs. Attrition: The analysis shows the distribution of attrition across different genders, providing insights into gender-specific attrition trends.

Model Evaluation

Various machine learning models were evaluated to predict employee attrition. The best model was identified based on its ROC AUC score, and the model was saved for future predictions.

Model Performance:

  • Logistic Regression
  • Gradient Boosting
  • Random Forest Classifier
  • Decision Tree Classifier
  • Naive Bayes
  • K-Nearest Neighbors
  • Support Vector Machine

The models were evaluated based on accuracy, ROC AUC score, confusion matrix, and classification report. The best-performing model was identified and saved for future use.

Conclusion

The analysis provides valuable insights into the factors contributing to employee attrition. Key factors such as age, years at the company, distance from home, job satisfaction, monthly income, years with the current manager, and years since the last promotion play a significant role in employee turnover. By addressing these factors, the company can develop targeted strategies to improve employee retention and reduce attrition rates.

Recommendations

  • Enhance Job Satisfaction: Implement programs to improve job satisfaction, such as recognition and reward systems, career development opportunities, and work-life balance initiatives.
  • Competitive Compensation: Review and adjust compensation packages to ensure they are competitive and aligned with industry standards.
  • Career Advancement: Provide clear career advancement paths and regular promotions to keep employees motivated and engaged.
  • Managerial Training: Train managers to build strong relationships with their team members and provide support and guidance.
  • Flexible Work Arrangements: Offer flexible work arrangements to reduce the impact of commute distance on employee retention.

By implementing these recommendations, the company can create a positive work environment, enhance employee satisfaction, and ultimately reduce attrition rates.
